# AOF Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Full Name:** The Ayotunde Oso Foundation  
**Abbreviated:** AOF  
**Tagline:** *Empowering Communities. Transforming Lives.*

## Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Empowering individuals and communities through education, healthcare, environmental sustainability, mentorship, and emergency humanitarian response.

**Vision:** A world where every individual has access to opportunities, quality healthcare, education, dignity, and hope.

## Core Values

1. **Integrity** — We do what we say. We say what we do.
2. **Compassion** — Every action rooted in genuine care.
3. **Transparency** — Radical openness in all we do.
4. **Accountability** — We measure and report our results.
5. **Innovation** — Creative solutions to persistent problems.
6. **Empowerment** — Building capacity, not dependency.
7. **Community** — Change from within, not imposed from outside.
8. **Inclusion** — No one left behind.
9. **Sustainability** — Programs built to outlast us.
10. **Service** — Putting communities first, always.

## Logo Usage

### Variations
- **Full logo:** Wordmark + symbol (primary use)
- **Symbol only:** For favicon, app icons, constrained spaces
- **Reversed:** White version for dark/green backgrounds

### Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the ‘A’ in ‘AOF’ on all sides of the logo.

### Minimum Sizes
- Digital: 120px wide (full logo), 32px wide (symbol)
- Print: 30mm wide (full logo), 8mm wide (symbol)

### Do Not
- Do not change the logo colors
- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without a clear area
- Do not use drop shadows on the logo
- Do not recreate the logo in a different font

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice Pillars

**Warm but not sentimental** — We are human and caring, but not emotionally manipulative.

**Clear and direct** — We communicate impact in plain language, not nonprofit jargon.

**Proud but humble** — We celebrate results without self-congratulation.

**Urgent but not alarmist** — We create a sense of importance without inducing panic.

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Fundraising appeals | Warm, specific, hopeful |
| Impact reports | Clear, data-driven, proud |
| Emergency response | Urgent, factual, action-oriented |
| Community stories | Human, intimate, celebratory |
| Legal/Policy | Formal, precise, neutral |

## Photography Style

- **People-centered:** Show real beneficiaries (with consent) with dignity and agency
- **Authentic:** Real moments, not staged stock photography
- **Bright and hopeful:** Natural light, warm tones when possible
- **Contextual:** Show programs in action, not just posed portraits
- **Diverse:** Reflect the full diversity of communities served
